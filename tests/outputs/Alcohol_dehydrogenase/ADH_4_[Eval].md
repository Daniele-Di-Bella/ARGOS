# Alcohol Dehydrogenases: Structure, Function, and Evolution

## Introduction

Alcohol dehydrogenases (ADHs) are a crucial family of enzymes that catalyze the reversible oxidation of alcohols to aldehydes or ketones while reducing a pyridine nucleotide, typically NAD+. These enzymes play significant roles in various metabolic pathways, particularly in the detoxification of ethanol in mammals and the fermentation processes in yeast and plants. Found in a wide spectrum of organisms, ADHs exhibit diverse structural characteristics and functional properties, influenced by evolutionary pressures across different lineages.
